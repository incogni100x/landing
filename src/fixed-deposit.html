Understanding Fixed Deposits

What is a Fixed Deposit?

A Fixed Deposit (FD) is a financial instrument provided by banks and financial institutions that offers investors a higher rate of interest than a regular savings account, until the given maturity date.

When you invest in a fixed deposit, you agree to lock in your money for a predetermined period, ranging from a few months to several years. In return, you receive a guaranteed interest rate that remains fixed throughout the deposit term.

Fixed deposits are considered one of the safest investment options as they provide assured returns and carry minimal risk. They are ideal for conservative investors looking for stability and security in their investment portfolio.

Portfolio Strategy

3 Reasons to Include FD in Your Portfolio

Strategic allocation of fixed deposits can significantly enhance your investment portfolio

1

Risk Diversification

Every investment portfolio needs a balance between high-risk and low-risk assets. Fixed deposits serve as the stable foundation of your investment strategy, providing a safety net against market volatility.

By allocating a portion of your portfolio to fixed deposits, you create a buffer that protects your overall investments from market downturns while ensuring steady growth.

Reduces overall portfolio risk
2

Predictable Income Stream

Fixed deposits provide a reliable source of income through regular interest payouts. This predictable cash flow can be particularly valuable for retirees or those seeking steady income without liquidating their investments.

With options for monthly, quarterly, or annual interest payouts, you can customize your fixed deposit to match your income requirements and financial planning.

Ensures financial stability
3

Goal-Based Investing

Fixed deposits are perfect for goal-based investing where you have a specific financial target with a defined timeline. Whether it's saving for a down payment, education, or a major purchase, FDs provide certainty.

By matching the FD tenure with your goal timeline, you can ensure that your funds grow steadily and become available exactly when you need them, without the uncertainty of market-linked investments.

Achieves financial goals with certainty

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our fixed deposit offerings

The minimum amount required to open a fixed deposit at Elite Mutual Fund is $1,000 for regular FDs and $10,000 for Tax Saver FDs. This low entry barrier makes it accessible for most investors to start their journey towards financial security.

Yes, you can withdraw your fixed deposit before maturity, subject to a nominal penalty. The premature withdrawal penalty is typically 1% of the applicable interest rate. However, Tax Saver FDs have a mandatory lock-in period of 5 years and cannot be withdrawn prematurely.

The interest earned on fixed deposits is taxable as per your income tax slab rate. TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) is applicable if the interest earned in a financial year exceeds ₹40,000 (₹50,000 for senior citizens). You can submit Form 15G/15H to avoid TDS if your total income is below the taxable limit.

Elite Mutual Fund offers multiple interest payout options to suit your financial needs:

  • Monthly Interest Payout: Ideal for regular income needs
  • Quarterly Interest Payout: For periodic income requirements
  • Annual Interest Payout: For yearly income planning
  • Cumulative (at maturity): For maximum returns through compounding

Yes, you can avail a loan of up to 90% of your fixed deposit amount. This is a great option when you need funds but don't want to break your FD and lose out on the interest. The loan interest rate is typically 2% higher than your FD interest rate, making it more cost-effective than many other loan options.

You have several options when your fixed deposit matures:

  • Withdraw the entire amount (principal + interest)
  • Renew the principal amount for another term
  • Renew the principal along with the accrued interest
  • Partially withdraw and renew the remaining amount

If no instruction is provided, the FD will be automatically renewed for the same tenure at the prevailing interest rate.
